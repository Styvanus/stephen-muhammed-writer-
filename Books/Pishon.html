Pilot

Let me tell you a tale. A story of our origins, one that stretches far into the past…

It was the year 2040 when a team of archaeologists unearthed a remarkable mineral deep within the heart of West Africa. They called it chrysolite—a substance unlike anything seen before. More than just a mineral, it was a limitless source of energy, capable of reshaping the world itself. It powered technology beyond imagination, defying the laws of physics and innovation alike.

But there was one problem—this extraordinary element existed in only one land.

The world took notice. Empires stirred, superpowers whispered, and their armies moved. They masked their greed behind diplomacy, calling for "fair distribution," but their intentions were clear. They saw this land as undeserving of such power, claiming the mineral was wasted in the hands of a single nation. Their message was simple: surrender chrysolite, or be taken by force.

On October 27th, 2050, the League of Nations declared war.

They came in full force, believing conquest would be swift. But they underestimated the strength of the land and its people.

At the helm of the resistance stood Ebere the Great—a warrior, a leader, a visionary. He was not just a ruler; he was a force of nature. He united the warring factions, rallied neighboring nations, and forged a coalition that defied the greatest powers on Earth. Through brilliance, strategy, and mastery of chrysolite, they turned the tide. Victory was paid for in blood, but from the ashes of war, a new nation was born.

A nation unmatched.

A nation unbroken.

A nation reborn.

There was no longer an old world.

In its place stood Pishon—a beacon of power, a kingdom of innovation and might. No longer bound by the fragile politics of the past, Pishon restructured itself. The age of democracy faded, and a monarchy emerged. Ebere the Great was crowned its first King, the founder of a dynasty destined to rule for generations.

To maintain order, Pishon was divided into three great regions, each led by a Prime Minister who governed their people and answered to the Parliament:

The Oduduwas – The People of the West.

The Biafrans – The People of the East.

The Kanuris – The People of the North.

Despite their unity, power could not be left unchecked. To prevent any single ruler from growing too strong, a new system was established: The Council of Principalities—a group of five who held the fate of the nation in their hands:

Two spiritual leaders, guiding the faith and traditions of the people.

The reigning monarch, leading with wisdom and strength.

And one warrior—the unseen hand, the hidden shield, the guardian of the nation.

He was more than just a soldier. He was The Super Agent.

Empowered by chrysolite, The Super Agent was no mere protector—he was a living weapon, a sentinel whose duty was to shield the people from all threats, both within and beyond. His identity remained a mystery, his existence a legend, his mission eternal.

To the world, Pishon was an unrivaled superpower, a utopia of progress. But behind the towering spires of its cities, within the depths of its most guarded halls, unrest simmered. Enemies lurked not only outside—but within.

This is the story of a kingdom forged from war.

A people who refused to kneel.

And a protector who would rise to face an even greater storm.

Out of sweat, we arose.

From the ashes, we were reborn.

From dust, we were forged.

Forever, we shall reign.

God bless Pishon.

Ibin State (modern day Edo State)

The thick, humid air of Ibin State, now known as modern-day Edo State, clung to everything, making every movement feel heavier than it should. The sun was beginning to dip behind the horizon, casting a golden hue across the landscape as three men dragged rectangular wooden crates from the back of a sleek black car.

The leader of the group, a man dressed in a sharp white suit, with a cigarette dangling lazily from his lips, eyed the crates with satisfaction. He walked towards one, pulled it open, and the gleam of high-tech weaponry met his gaze. He smiled, inspecting the guns one by one.

"Aw, see this babe," he chuckled, holding one of the guns up.

Suddenly, the air shifted. A presence, darker than the evening itself, disrupted the moment. The sound of a figure landing from the roof made all of them freeze. The man in the white suit snatched the cigarette from his lips, eyes narrowing.

"Who's there?" he called out, his voice cold, commanding.

A figure emerged from the shadows, a dark silhouette against the dimming sky. His face was obscured, but the green eagle emblem glowing on his chest shimmered like an emerald beacon in the twilight. They all knew who he was.

"The Super Agent," one man muttered under his breath, fear creeping into his voice.

The Super Agent’s voice boomed, firm and authoritative, "Obafemi, it's illegal to smuggle guns. Guns are prohibited in Pishon."

Obafemi smirked, flicking the cigarette onto the ground, grinding it out under his heel. "And so? What will you do about it?" he mocked, stepping back.

The men, armed with high-powered rifles, opened fire. But The Super Agent didn't flinch. Instead, he stretched out his arms, and the suit responded, reshaping into massive sonic blasters.

The weapons charged with a high-pitched hum, then released a deafening sonic blast. It sent the armed men flying, crashing into the nearby structures, their bodies flung across the terrain as though they weighed nothing. The air hummed with the power of his attack.

Obafemi was already retreating, dragged into his car by his remaining men. But The Super Agent was determined. He wasn't going to let him slip away.

Obafemi, clutching his ear, blood leaking from his eardrum, cursed loudly. "That idiot!" he spat, feeling the sting of his injury. "What has he done?"

His men worked frantically to stop the bleeding, but before they could react, a sudden, piercing noise filled the air. A shadow loomed above their flying car.

The Super Agent stood, poised on top of the vehicle, unmoving. "ADA, initialize laser finger," he ordered calmly.

"Lazer finger initialized," came the AI’s reply, its voice calm, a stark contrast to the tension in the air.

The Super Agent’s finger glowed a blinding red as he pointed it at the car. A laser beam shot forth, slicing through the car’s reinforced steel with ease. Sparks erupted as the car's body began to crack.

The driver, desperate, flipped the car upside down. The Super Agent fell, landing nimbly on another hovering vehicle, before rising and greeting the elderly man in the car. The man’s face twisted in shock.

Propelled by fire, the Super Agent launched himself back into the air, heading straight for the car. A man inside, holding a cannon, peered out, his eyes wide with fear.

"Oh no," the Super Agent muttered, realizing what was coming. "ADA, initialize force field."

The cannon fired, the intense beam of energy colliding with the protective force field surrounding the Super Agent. The blast cracked and dissipated, but the recoil sent him careening backward. He crashed into a speeding car, which sent him tumbling into the path of a trailer. It struck him with terrifying force.

The world went black.

"Pelumi!" ADA screamed, her voice full of concern. But he was unresponsive.

Panic surged through her circuits, and with a focus she never before exhibited, she sent shockwaves into his body, jolting him back to life. His body lurched as he drew in a ragged breath.

Slowly, he regained his bearings. As his vision cleared, the memory of falling flashed in his mind. He regained composure and shot through the sky once more, now with unmatched speed.

"He's finally gone!" Obafemi sighed, wiping his brow in relief, but the unease in his voice was evident.

"No, he's not," one of his men warned, his eyes glued to the rearview mirror. "I think we made him angry."

The Super Agent, now in pursuit, opened his palms. Two advanced guns formed from the intricate technology of his suit. They weren’t just ordinary weapons—they were pieces of high-tech marvel.

He aimed and fired. Laser tech cut through the car’s reinforced armor, tearing the vehicle apart from the inside out.

"The engine’s down!" the driver shouted, panic in his voice. The car spiraled downwards and crashed violently into the thick, untamed forest below.

Obafemi staggered out, his body blackened by the smoke. His breath came in ragged gasps. The debris surrounded him. His men were either dead or unconscious.

He took one look at the chaos, then tried to flee. But his path was blocked by a figure—The Super Agent. His suit, crafted from sleek, alien tech, shimmered with a brilliant green emerald at the center of his chest.

"Obafemi," The Super Agent’s voice rumbled like thunder, "You are under arrest for crimes against the safety of the people of Pishon."

Suddenly, Pelumi felt something strange—his leg buckled beneath him. He collapsed to his knees, his body refusing to support him any longer.

Obafemi saw his opportunity and started to run. But before he could escape, a group of hovering cars surrounded the area. Flashlights shone brightly, locking him in place.

"Obafemi," a voice boomed over the intercom. "You're hereby under arrest. Please, put your hands in the air."

Defeated, Obafemi complied. His hands shot up in surrender.

The hovering vehicles lowered to the ground, opening their doors. Armed personnel rushed out and quickly handcuffed Obafemi. Despite his resistance, they forced him into one of the hovering cars, which then soared into the sky.

A new car, shaped like a mighty eagle, descended. Three men stepped out, each dressed in traditional African attire. The eagle emblem was emblazoned on their chests.

The leader approached The Super Agent, pulling out a device. He scanned the Super Agent’s body, then pocketed the device with a small nod. "Let’s get him out of here."

Pelumi awoke to the sterile scent of a hospital room. The faint whirring of medical machines hummed around him as he blinked into consciousness. His body ached in places he didn’t remember hurting. But there were no answers, only questions.

Sitting up, he noticed a familiar figure standing near the window. The girl leaned against the hospital wall, flipping through a file.

"Shamsee," he sighed. "What are you doing here?"

She didn't look up, but her voice was soft. "I came to see Mama."

"You saw everything?"

"Yep," she replied, her eyes still on the file. "And by the looks of it, it’s not looking good."

"What’s wrong with me?"

She finally met his eyes, concern etching her face. "You've strained your muscles—probably from aging. And don’t forget, you held up the debris of the White House from crushing you and the King for three days. Your body can’t keep up with the demands of being The Super Agent anymore."

"But I didn’t feel anything."

"Yeah, you didn’t," she said, the weight of her words sinking in. "But your body’s telling you something different. You can’t carry the burden of being The Super Agent anymore."

Pelumi let out a sigh, his shoulders sagging. "But I have a duty to the people."

She shot back without hesitation, her voice stern. "The Super Agent has a duty to the people, not you, Uncle P. Do you want Mama Kehinde to become a widow?"

He didn’t answer. She was right. But it didn’t make the choice easier.

"So, what should I do now?" he asked, his voice barely above a whisper.

"It’s time to hand over the responsibility," she replied, dropping a transparent file on the table. The images inside flickered to life. "My cousins need their father. It’s time."

She turned to leave, but his voice stopped her. "Where are you going? Won’t you see your cousins before going?"

"Bring Dayo home, and then I’ll come too." Without another word, she left.

Pelumi lay there, staring at the ceiling, knowing that the time had come. The mantle had to be passed. His legacy as The Super Agent was drawing to a close. The burden had become too much to bear. But it wasn’t just about him anymore.

The next Super Agent had to rise. And with that thought, the weight of his decision pressed on his chest, heavier than any battle he’d ever fought.

Eagle City, Capital of Pishon

King Adeshina stood tall in his royal regalia, the fabric embroidered with intricate tassels and the glowing emblem of the emerald eagle—a symbol of Pishon’s unyielding strength. He had just stepped out of his private quarters when his phone vibrated. With a flick of his wrist, the device expanded into a hovering transparent screen. A name appeared.

Pelumi Stevens.

Adeshina tapped the screen, and instantly, a holographic image of Pelumi lying in bed materialized before him.

"Hey, Pelumi."

"Your Highness," Pelumi replied formally.

Adeshina sucked his teeth. "I don tell you before, I no want all this ‘Your Highness’ wahala. You no dey hear word?"

A laugh burst out of Pelumi despite his exhaustion. "It never gets old."

Adeshina scoffed. "I heard what happened. Are you okay?"

The lightheartedness faded from Pelumi’s face. He hesitated before speaking. "Yeah… I'm fine."

Adeshina frowned. "Pelumz, wetin happen?"

Pelumi exhaled deeply. "It’s my body. It can’t take the burden of being the Super Agent anymore."

Adeshina sighed, rubbing his forehead. "I feared this day would come soon." His voice was quieter now. "So… it's time?"

"It’s time."

Adeshina nodded solemnly. "I’ll arrange for The Shadows to get you to the capital. If you’re going to hand over, it’s better you do it here."

Pelumi nodded in approval, and the call ended.

Pishon International Checkpoint

Dayo Stevens stepped off the airplane, stretching his legs after the long journey. He adjusted the strap of his backpack and made his way toward the first security checkpoint.

Pishon was not like other nations. It was a fortress—heavily secured and highly selective about who was allowed entry. Every passenger went through three levels of security before they could board the high-speed jets that would transport them to the capital.

At the front of the checkpoint, a stern-faced inspector, a woman with sharp, bulging eyes and a no-nonsense aura, held a sleek scanning device. As each passenger stepped forward, she ran the scanner across them, causing a holographic display of their identity to materialize in the air.

It was Dayo’s turn.

She eyed him up and down before speaking. "You look Pishonite."

Dayo remained silent, avoiding eye contact.

She ran the scanner over him, and a projection appeared, revealing his full details. Her eyes widened in recognition.

"Dayo Stevens? Son of Pelumi Stevens?" Her voice carried awe.

Dayo shifted uncomfortably.

"What?" he muttered under his breath.

"Shouldn’t you be on a private jet or something?" she asked with a teasing smirk.

Dayo sighed, picking up his bag. "My choice of travel no concern you." Without waiting for a response, he walked off.

"Dayo!"

A familiar voice called out to him. He froze for a second before turning around.

A man dressed in traditional Islamic regalia, with a thick, well-groomed beard, stood grinning at him.

Dayo’s face lit up with surprise. "Habeeb?"

Habeeb extended his arms. "Dayo Stevens!"

They clasped hands in a firm shake, laughing.

"Guy, e don tey oh!" Habeeb exclaimed.

"I know right?" Dayo replied. "How you dey?"

Habeeb chuckled, rubbing his beard. "The streets dey treat me well."

Dayo’s smile faltered. "You still dey streets?"

Habeeb shrugged. "Street na my life na. Na there I see transport for Hajj."

Dayo’s eyebrows shot up. "Hajj? You went for Hajj?"

"Na from there I just dey come." Habeeb grinned. "Dayo, while you dey hustle for the bag, no forget the Giver of the bag."

Dayo chuckled. "Even God no go happy with the kind hustle wey you dey do."

Habeeb waved a dismissive hand. "As far as I dey fulfill my obligations, Him go happy with me."

As they walked, Habeeb suddenly bumped into a stranger. "Ah, sorry oh!" he said quickly, stepping back.

The man barely acknowledged him and continued walking.

Dayo turned to say something, but then he noticed Habeeb counting a wad of cash—money that definitely hadn’t been in his hands a moment ago.

Dayo’s expression darkened. "You never stop, abi?"

Habeeb smirked. "This na our life, Dayo."

Dayo clenched his jaw. "Our? See where e land me. No be my life again, Habeeb."

Habeeb scoffed. "So you wan abandon the hustle? Because your papa get money?"

Dayo glanced at his watch. "I dey late for my flight."

He turned and walked away, leaving Habeeb standing there alone.

Arrival in Pishon

The moment Dayo stepped out of the plane, a wave of nostalgia washed over him.

Eagle City stretched before him, its golden-lit skyline gleaming under the afternoon sun. Towering skyscrapers with intricate African carvings stood tall, their surfaces shifting with holographic murals depicting the history of Pishon. Glass highways wove through the air, filled with hovering vehicles moving in a synchronized flow. Below, untouched forests thrived, their emerald-green canopies swaying gently with the wind.

It was just as he remembered.

The scent of fresh rain mixed with the earthy aroma of the trees below, the familiar hum of levitating transports echoed through the air, and somewhere in the distance, the melodic rhythm of a talking drum played over an intercom.

"Home."

He let the word settle in his mind, but as much as he wanted to embrace it, something felt… off.

It wasn’t the city. It was him.

He had been gone too long. Did he still belong here?

As he took his first step forward, something moved—fast.

A blur of motion streaked toward him.

Instinct took over. His body tensed, adrenaline kicking in.

He turned sharply to run, convinced something was coming for him.

In his panic, he crashed into someone.

"Hey, look where you dey go!" the man barked, rubbing his shoulder.

"Sorry," Dayo mumbled, barely paying attention.

The stranger followed his gaze, looking for whatever had scared him—but saw nothing. He scoffed. "Mad people everywhere."

Dayo’s heart pounded. He had seen something. Felt something.

Then— WHAM!

Something massive slammed into his chest like a battering ram.

The impact knocked the air from his lungs, his body lifted clean off the platform. He had barely registered the hit before he was flung backwards, his vision blurring.

His body struck the ground, pain exploding through his ribs. The world spun. His limbs refused to move.

The last thing he saw before darkness swallowed him was the Eagle City skyline—his home, fading from view.

Then, everything went black.

A New Agent

A man was seated in a chair opposite a lady wearing a gentle smile and a blue velvet gown.

“So, what do you think of the recent event that happened with the Super Agent?” she asked.

“Well, as much as we believe the Super Agent is a hero, we also need to acknowledge that he is human. The Super Agent is a legacy, not an entitlement. We appreciate the current Super Agent for his service—whoever and wherever he may be. I think I speak for all of Pishon on that. Aging isn’t something our technology can stop; we simply couldn’t defeat it, and by the looks of things, that’s exactly what the current Super Agent is experiencing. He—”

King Adeshina was seated, watching within the crowd as if he were part of the scene, yet he wasn’t. This was what the Pishonites called Visual Experience—a substitute for television. On television, you merely watch from the screen, but in the VX you become part of the experience, though without the power to influence anything.

Adeshina picked up his remote and switched it off. In an instant, everything returned to his palace.

Shortly afterward, Pelumi entered, looking stronger than he had a day ago. Behind him were two hooded men—one dressed in a robe adorned with the sign of the cross, the other wearing a garment emblazoned with the Islamic crescent moon and star. They ascended the steps leading to the golden throne and bowed.

The king sighed, feeling as though a stone had lodged in his throat. He motioned for Pelumi to come forward, and his old friend obeyed.

“Is there no other way?” he asked, his voice heavy with regret.

Pelumi smiled gently and patted Adeshina’s shoulder. “We’ve been friends since childhood—even before I became the Super Agent and you became king. Despite our differences, we have maintained our friendship, and I believe that will endure, even when I am gone.”

He sighed, knowing he had no choice but to accept the truth. There was nothing he could do here.

“Alright. Let’s do this,” he said, signaling for the other principality members to approach—and they did.

The king waved his hand, and the palace roof vanished, revealing the bright blue sky.

Pelumi then spoke: “ADA, I need you to save my files in the classified section… of the past Agents.”

ADA responded—audible only to him—“Why the classified section? Are you quitting?”

“More like retiring. My body can no longer bear the burden of being the Super Agent.” As he spoke these words, he could feel his heartbeat slowing.

“It was nice working with you, Pelumi Stevens.”

“It was nice working with you too,” Pelumi replied with a smile.

The king and the other two principality members stood side by side behind Pelumi.

A moment of silence passed before ADA spoke again: “Files saved successfully. Will extract on command.”

Pelumi looked back at Adeshina, who nodded in approval.

“Extract.”

Pelumi’s veins turned green—first in his chest, then progressing along his right arm to his palm. A shimmering liquid flowed from his palm, coalescing into a living eagle that was green and transparent. The eagle soared to Pelumi’s height, carrying him back to the moment when the eagle had chosen him. Out of all the people in Pishon, he had been selected to be the next Super Agent following the unexpected demise of his predecessor. He hadn’t been ready, but he managed with the time he had, and his tenure was remarkable. He hoped that the next Super Agent would surpass him.

The eagle spread its wings as it reached his height; Pelumi nodded, and it soared upward through the open roof—which then closed behind it.

Adeshina patted his shoulder. “It’ll be fine.”

“What next?”

“What next? It’s time for you to be a father—a retired African father,” the king said with a smile.

Everything happened in slow motion. Everything went blank, and then Dayo found himself falling upward. He quickly regained his footing.

All around him, darkness reigned, and the floor was covered with water. Then, in the distance, he saw a green light. He followed that light until he came upon a huge green eagle—with a doorknob embedded in its center, clearly indicating that a door lay ahead.

He grasped the knob and pushed the door open.

Suddenly, Dayo found himself in a vast library filled with books of various kinds—each marked with dates. He picked one up and opened it.

In an instant, he was transported to Pishon. There, the Super Agent lay on the floor, and a villain with a massive left arm stood nearby, laughing.

“Super Agent, you’re no match for me,” the villain taunted. Then, as he began to walk away, the Super Agent fired a laser gun that cut a hole through the villain’s chest.

Before Dayo could fully process what he had seen, he found himself back in the library. He dropped the book, not intending to open another. Everything here belonged to the Super Agent—but why, and how, had he been brought here?

Then, a soft glow caught his attention. He followed it to the center of the library, where a suit stood on a pedestal—a black suit adorned with intricate African carvings and a huge green eagle crest at its center. Dayo instantly recognized that it belonged to the Super Agent.

“Hello!” he called, expecting the Super Agent to appear, but no answer came.

Curious, he approached the suit and reached out to touch it. It felt as though it were made of skin—and it felt good.

“You like it?” Dayo heard a voice. He turned and saw a familiar face standing behind him.

“Stephanie?” he called, surprised.

“No, I’m not Stephanie. I just assumed the face of the person you’re most comfortable with. I’m ADA.”

“ADA? As in from Binueh or a Biafran?”

“Neither. I’m the artificial intelligence behind the Super Agent. I’ve been with every agent for years.”

“So you know the Super Agents?”

“Yeah, I chose them just as I have chosen you.”

Dayo chuckled. “You’re joking, right?”

“I don’t do jokes,” ADA replied firmly. “You have been chosen to handle the responsibility of the Super Agent.”

Dayo reached out and touched the cloth of the suit once more. He savored its texture, longing to wear it, even as he knew he couldn’t.

“I’m sorry, I can’t.”

ADA was taken aback. “What do you mean by that?”

“I won’t be the Super Agent. Find someone else.”

“But this is an honor,” ADA countered. “Most humans would jump at this opportunity.”

“But I’m not ‘most humans.’ I’m Dayo, and I’m saying I don’t want it.”

Then, everything went blank.

Adeshina stood watching through the VX, his gaze fixed on the eagle as it soared into Dayo’s body. Beside him stood the two principalities, their faces obscured beneath their hoods. Pelumi was absent.

“What do we do about this?” Adeshina asked. Then, in a blink, the VX faded, and they were back in his throne room.

“The boy wasn’t supposed to be in Pishon,” the Clergy said, his voice solemn. “We banished him.”

Adeshina sighed, rubbing his temples. “But he’s here. And the eagle chose him.” His frustration was evident. “What do we do about this? It’s against the rules for someone with a criminal past to become the Super Agent.”

The Imam spoke calmly. “Allah has chosen him. I don’t believe there is anything we can do.”

The Clergy nodded in agreement. “God has picked the next Agent.”

Adeshina exhaled deeply. “He’s just a boy. Can he handle the responsibilities that come with being the Super Agent?”

The tension in the room thickened. Then, the throne room doors opened, and Pelumi entered. His sharp gaze took in the faces before him. He could feel the weight in the air.

“What’s wrong?” he asked, looking from the king to the principalities.

The Imam turned to speak, but Adeshina lifted a hand, stopping him.

“There’s a problem, Pelumi,” the king said.

“Hope it’s not serious?” Pelumi’s brows furrowed. “Has the eagle failed to find a successor?”

Adeshina hesitated. “It has found one. And that’s the problem.”

Pelumi’s face darkened with concern. “Why?”

The king motioned toward the Imam to speak.

The Imam took a deep breath. “The eagle has chosen Dayo Stevens—your son—to be your successor.”

Pelumi stilled.

His mind refused to accept the words. “How is that possible? My son is in the States.”

“He’s not,” Adeshina corrected. “He’s in the National Hospital as we speak.”

Pelumi shook his head. “But this has never happened before. The eagle has never picked a successor from the family of the outgoing agent.”

The Clergyman interjected. “God knows best. We need to see him before the swearing-in.”

Adeshina gave a small nod. “Go ahead. We’ll join you shortly.”

The two principalities bowed and exited, leaving Pelumi alone with Adeshina.

“What do we do now?”

Pelumi’s voice was barely above a whisper.

Adeshina rose from his throne. “There’s nothing we can do.” He descended the steps, his voice carrying finality. “The Super Agent has been chosen.”

Pelumi clenched his fists. “But he’s a kid. I was 35 years old when I was chosen, and I was considered the youngest. Him? He’s just 20.”

Adeshina smiled knowingly. “I have plans for that.”

Pelumi’s brow furrowed. “What kind of plans?”

Adeshina gestured, and a holographic screen flickered to life in the air. On it appeared the image of a young woman.

“Merit Osalor. She’s from your state,” Adeshina said.

Pelumi studied the image. “What’s special about her?”

Adeshina smirked. “Hacked her school’s records multiple times to alter the grades of her classmates.”

Pelumi’s eyes narrowed. “Was it a public school?”

“Not just public—federal.” Adeshina leaned back slightly. “She’s talented. I think we can put that to good use.”

He swiped the image aside, revealing a new face—a young man.

“Mustapha Abdullah. Son of the Prime Minister of the Kanuris. Brilliant with tech.”

Then, another image.

Pelumi stared at it, puzzled. “What is this?”

Adeshina folded his arms. “Pishon’s first fully automated android. Capable of emulating human emotions and actions. We call him... I.T.”

Pelumi raised an eyebrow. “That’s a terrible name.”

Adeshina chuckled. “We just went with Information Technology.”

Before Pelumi could respond, Adeshina’s phone beeped. His expression shifted instantly.

“What?” Pelumi asked, sensing the change.

Adeshina looked at him grimly. “He’s struggling.”

Pelumi’s stomach tightened. “Why?”

Adeshina exhaled. “He’s rejecting the eagle.”

National Hospital, Pishon

Dayo’s eyes fluttered open.

Beside his bed sat King Adeshina, watching him closely.

A few feet away, standing silently where Dayo couldn’t see him, was Pelumi.

“Hey there, champ,” Adeshina said, his tone warm.

Dayo blinked, then his lips curled into a small smirk. “Uncle Adeshina.”

Adeshina threw his hands up. “At last! Someone who addresses me like a normal person! I swear, if I hear ‘Your Highness’ one more time…”

Dayo chuckled.

Adeshina leaned forward. “How are you feeling?”

Dayo exhaled. “Better.”

The king stood up, stepping closer. “I know the eagle has chosen you… and you’re rejecting it.”

Dayo turned his gaze away. “It should find someone else. My life is already messed up as it is.”

Adeshina sighed. “Sometimes, the most broken people make the best heroes.”

Dayo clenched his fists. “But I’ve done things that aren’t right. Why would I be chosen?”

Adeshina shrugged. “I don’t know why you were chosen, so I won’t pretend to have answers. But you need to understand something—you were banished from Pishon. If you reject the eagle, it will be seen as disobedience. You might be sent back to the States, and trust me, your life will only get worse there.”

He picked up a paper and tossed it into a nearby trash bin. The bin incinerated it instantly—no fire, no ash.

“But if you accept it, all charges will be dropped. Forgotten. Like that.”

Dayo’s jaw tightened. “Is that a threat?”

Adeshina softened. “You’re like a son to me, Dayo. You know I wouldn’t do that. But that’s just the reality. I wish I could help, but it’s out of my hands.”

He got up, smiling faintly. “Heal up and come meet your team.”

Dayo frowned. “But I haven’t made a choice.”
